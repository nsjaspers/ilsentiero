Sanctuary & Retreats · Salento, Puglia

A path.
Not a program.

Every retreat is built around you — your energy, your pace, your readiness to go deep.

The foundation

Structure as a riverbank.

Il Sentiero was born from a simple but profound realisation: you cannot receive healing if you are never still.

For years I worked intuitively with people — tuning into their energy, following the flow, being fully present. And I gave everything. Sometimes too much. It took hosting my first private retreats to understand that sustainable depth requires structure. Not rigid schedules. A gentle container.

A riverbank that gives the water direction without controlling where it flows. That is what Il Sentiero is. A path. Not a program.

"You cannot receive healing
if you are never still."

The rhythm

A day at Il Sentiero.

Each day has a natural rhythm that creates the conditions for real transformation to happen. This is not a schedule — it is a container. Within it, everything is alive and responsive to you.

Early morning

Before we meet

I tend to my own practice.

Before our day begins, I arrive to the work grounded and present. I can only hold space as well as I hold myself. This is not a small thing — it is the foundation of everything.

Morning

The ritual

We begin together in silence.

After breakfast, we gather. A candle. Oracle cards. Your journal. We breathe together, slow down, and open the day with three questions. You write. You keep everything you write. By the end of the retreat, you leave with a full record of your own inner journey.

Late morning

Into nature

We go out — together.

Into the land, into the village, wherever feels right. The wild coastline, the olive groves, the sea. The connection with nature is a big part of this journey. Once we open ourselves to this land, we remember how supported we are by the elements around us.

Midday

Lunch

Long and unhurried.

This too is part of the work. Sitting together, eating slowly, letting the morning settle. There is nothing to rush toward. This is the retreat.

Afternoon

Alone time

This is not empty time.

After lunch, we each take time alone. Space to integrate, to rest, to simply be. This is intentional — this is the retreat working. What moves in the morning continues moving in the silence of the afternoon.

Late afternoon

The work

Whatever is needed.

A deeper therapeutic session, a walk, a visit to one of my trusted practitioner friends in the area, or simply being. In the morning ritual, we sense together what the day is calling for. The afternoon answers that call.

Evening · Fire

The fire

The fire changes everything.

There is no agenda around the fire. No method. You sit. The flames move. The waves are always there in the background. And something in you — slowly, quietly — begins to soften.

Fire has a way of doing what words cannot. It makes people honest. It makes people still. In that stillness, what has been waiting inside you finally has room to rise.

Sometimes we light it in the evening. Sometimes in the afternoon, just because the moment calls for it. There is no rule. Only the fire, the sea, and whatever needs to come up. On a clear night, the full moon above — or a new moon, and every star visible.

Evening

By 9pm

The day closes gently.

A light meal, slow conversation, maybe the sunset over the Adriatic. By 9pm, the day is yours. Rest is part of the work too. Tomorrow begins again — fresh, unhurried, present.

The morning ritual

Three questions.
Every morning.

These are not therapy questions. They are not performance questions. They are invitations — to arrive, to listen, to give yourself permission.

01

"How do you arrive this morning?"

In three words. No more. Not a story — just three words. This trains presence from the very first moment.

02

"What is asking for your attention today?"

Not what you want to work on. What is asking. The body, the emotion, the thing underneath — invited to speak.

03

"What would feel like a gift to yourself today?"

Quietly radical for people who overgive. A seed of self-permission planted at the start of every day.

+ A fourth, rotating question — or an oracle card. Whatever the day is calling for.

This is not for you if

—

You are waiting for the perfect moment. There is no perfect moment.

—

You want someone to fix you, tell you what to do, or give you all the answers.

—

You are not truly ready to look at what has been holding you back.

—

You are looking for a holiday with some wellness on the side. This is not that.

—

You keep making excuses for why now is not the right time. I am not the right guide for that.
